# Commander Brackets

Use this context when discussing power level or recommending cards for Commander decks. The Bracket system helps players find pods that match their desired game experience.

*Source: [EDHREC Guide to Commander Brackets](https://edhrec.com/guides/edhrec-guide-to-commander-brackets) (Cooper Gottfried). Most recent update: October 22, 2025.*

---

## What Are Commander Brackets?

The Bracket system is a way for Commander players to ensure they're sitting down for the kind of game they want to play. When Wizards of the Coast took over the Commander format in 2024, they promised a deck categorization system for easier pre-game conversations. Brackets help casual players, competitive players, and everyone in between find like-minded pods.

---

## The Five Brackets

From most casual to most competitive:

| Bracket | Name       | Typical game length |
|---------|------------|----------------------|
| 1       | Exhibition | 9+ turns             |
| 2       | Core       | 8+ turns             |
| 3       | Upgraded   | 6+ turns             |
| 4       | Optimized  | 4+ turns             |
| 5       | cEDH       | Any turn             |

Lower brackets include less powerful cards, fewer combos, and are often more theme-focused. Higher brackets feature meta-adherent, efficient wins and optimized consistency.

---

## Bracket 1 (Exhibition)

**Players expect:**
- Decks to prioritize a goal, theme, or idea over power
- Rules around card or commander legality to be flexible, if all players agree
- Win conditions to be highly thematic or substandard
- Gameplay to be an opportunity to show off their creations
- At least nine turns before anyone wins or loses

**Deck restrictions** (exceptions can be made for highly thematic cards):
- No Game Changers
- No mass land denial
- No extra turns
- No two-card combos (game-enders, lockouts, or infinites)

**Deckbuilding:** Theme above all else. Cards chosen for flavor, art, or creative constraints. Minimal tutors, suboptimal card advantage. Example: a deck where every card is a Bear, has a bear in its art, or mentions "bear" in rules text.

---

## Bracket 2 (Core)

**Players expect:**
- Decks to be mechanically focused with some cards chosen for creativity and/or entertainment
- Win conditions to be incremental, telegraphed on the board, or disruptable
- Gameplay to be low pressure, proactive, and considerate—letting each deck showcase its plan
- At least eight turns before anyone wins or loses

**Deck restrictions:**
- No Game Changers
- No mass land denial
- No chaining extra turns
- No two-card combos (game-enders, lockouts, or infinites)

**Deckbuilding:** Thematic but with stronger game pieces. Some tutors, graveyard synergies, and cards that work well with the commander. Still a theme at the core, but actually trying to win.

---

## Bracket 3 (Upgraded)

**Players expect:**
- Decks to be powered up with strong synergy and high card quality; can effectively disrupt opponents
- Win conditions that can be played from hand in one turn, usually from steadily accrued resources
- Gameplay to feature many proactive and reactive plays
- At least six turns before anyone wins or loses

**Deck restrictions:**
- Up to three Game Changers
- No mass land denial
- No chaining extra turns
- No two-card combos before turn six

**Deckbuilding:** Cohesive strategy over theme. Strong synergy, utility lands, tutors, and a defined path to victory. Willing to use powerful cards but not necessarily free spells or busted one-offs. Cohesive game plan apparent across card groups.

---

## Bracket 4 (Optimized)

**Players expect:**
- Decks to be lethal, consistent, and fast, designed to take people down as fast as possible
- Decks do not adhere to the cEDH metagame
- Win conditions to vary by archetype but can end a game quickly and suddenly
- Gameplay to be explosive and powerful, with huge threats and efficient disruption
- At least four turns before anyone wins or loses

**Deck restrictions:** None other than the banned list.

**Deckbuilding:** Bracket 3 supercharged. Commander's most powerful cards, more tutors, efficient disruption, free spells. Combos are common and overlapping. Account for opponents' countermagic and game plans. Pet cards often cut for consistency.

---

## Bracket 5 (cEDH)

**Players expect:**
- Decks meticulously designed to battle in the cEDH metagame
- Ability to win quickly or generate overwhelming resources
- Often built using existing cEDH knowledge, tools, and/or deck lists
- Win conditions optimized for efficiency and consistency
- Gameplay to be intricate and advanced, with razor-thin margins for error
- Players prioritize victory over all else
- Games can end on any turn

**Deck restrictions:** None other than the banned list.

**Deckbuilding:** Maximum optimization. Turbo (fast combo) or stax (slow opponents) strategies. Many overlapping combos, dense tutor density, free interaction, fast mana. Built for the cEDH metagame.

---

## Choosing Your Bracket

**Which bracket is right for me?** Most players start at Bracket 2 or Bracket 3—room for creative expression without constant optimization pressure. A store-bought precon or personal collection can jump into Bracket 2.

**My deck could fit in Bracket X or Y!** The difference comes down to **deckbuilding intent**. The Bracket System guides pre-game discussions; it is not gospel. It's okay to deviate—have honest discussions with your pod before the game.

**Game Changers:** EDHREC maintains a [Game Changers](https://edhrec.com/top/game-changers) list of high-impact cards. Bracket 1–2 avoid them; Bracket 3 allows up to three.
